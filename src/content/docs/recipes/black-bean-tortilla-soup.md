---
title: Black Bean Tortilla Soup with Sweet Potatoes
description: A hearty vegetarian tortilla soup with black beans, sweet potatoes, and crispy homemade tortilla strips. From Love Real Food by Kathryne Taylor.
---

## Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons plus 2 teaspoons extra-virgin olive oil, divided
- 1 large yellow onion, chopped
- 1 small sweet potato (about ½ lb), peeled and cut into ½-inch dice
- 1 medium red bell pepper, chopped
- 1 medium jalapeño, seeded, deribbed, and finely chopped
- Fine sea salt
- 4 cloves garlic, pressed or minced
- 2 teaspoons ground cumin
- 1½ teaspoons chili powder
- 2 cans (15 oz each) black beans, rinsed and drained (or 3 cups cooked)
- 1 can (14.5 oz) diced fire-roasted tomatoes, with liquid
- 4 cups vegetable broth
- 6 corn tortillas, cut into 2-inch-long, ½-inch-wide strips
- ½ cup chopped fresh cilantro, divided
- 1 tablespoon lime juice
- ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
- Freshly ground black pepper

**Garnishes**
- 2 radishes, thinly sliced and roughly chopped
- ½ cup crumbled feta cheese (about 1½ oz)

## Instructions

1. In a large Dutch oven, warm 2 tablespoons of the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, sweet potato, bell pepper, jalapeño, and ½ teaspoon salt. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the onions are translucent and the sweet potatoes can be pierced easily with a fork, 10–15 minutes.
2. Stir in the garlic, cumin, and chili powder and cook until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Pour in the beans, tomatoes, and broth. Increase heat to medium-high and bring to a simmer. Cook, reducing the heat as needed to maintain a gentle simmer, until the broth is flavorful and the sweet potatoes and beans are very tender, about 30 minutes.
3. Meanwhile, preheat the oven to 400°F. Line a large baking sheet with parchment paper.
4. On the baking sheet, toss the tortilla strips with the remaining 2 teaspoons olive oil until lightly coated. Spread in a single layer, sprinkle with salt, and bake until crisp, about 8 minutes, flipping halfway through.
5. Once the soup is done, stir in most of the cilantro (reserving about 1 tablespoon) and the lime juice. Season to taste with salt, pepper flakes, and black pepper.
6. To serve, divide about half the tortilla strips among the bowls. Ladle the soup over the strips. Top with the remaining tortilla strips, radishes, feta, and a sprinkle of the reserved cilantro.

Makes 5–8 hearty bowls.
